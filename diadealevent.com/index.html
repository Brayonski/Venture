VentureLift Africa

Based in Nairobi, we are an advisory company, fintech platform and curated marketplace. We help the Africa Diaspora and other international users engage top startups and growing SMEs across Africa for investment, trade, supply chain procurement, technology transfer, business services and talent recruitment. The global Africa Diaspora totals nearly 140 million and around 46 million in the United States.

We aim to curate into Africa more Diaspora engagement beyond remittances and create a stronger economic bridge focusing on the regional and international growth of businesses.

VLA provides capital providers (equity, debt and donation crowdfunding) with vetted investment opportunities and connections to qualified transaction advisors and consultants with local knowledge and track record in Africa.

Finally, through our in-person and Knowledge Center education modules, VLA provides instruction and training on a comprehensive range of topics for entrepreneurs, investors and managers, including fundraising, governance, due diligence in frontier markets, mergers and acquisitions, operations management and more.

Dr. Wilmot Allen

Based in Nairobi, Wilmot is an experienced emerging market investor, business advisor, entrepreneur and Africa political economist. As Founder, Wilmot launched VLA in 2019 inspired by his work in the U.S. and engagement with over 20 African countries during the last two decades.

Wilmot is also a Venture Partner with RH Managers, a private equity fund investing in healthcare, based in Johannesburg. He previously worked as Director of East Africa with an U.S. investment firm, CrossBoundary LLC, leading the transaction advisory practice for East Africa from Nairobi. Under his leadership, this group served as the transaction team for a USAID initiative, the East Africa Trade and Investment Hub. The team facilitated over $110 million in closed transactions in only 24 months and established the firm’s most successful investment advisory platform.

Prior, Wilmot worked with the International Finance Corporation in Washington DC, executing investments totaling over $300 million and managing a portfolio of $2.5 billion with the private equity and financial institutions groups. At the IFC, he was also Senior Advisor to the CEO on entrepreneurship initiatives in Africa, Asia and the Middle East, in conjunction with the Global Agenda Council on Youth Unemployment at the World Economic Forum.

In other roles, Wilmot worked as a venture capital investor with the Commonwealth Enterprise Fund in Boston, a technology and media M&A investment banker with JPMorgan H&Q in San Francisco and a private placement and IPO investment banker with Merrill Lynch in New York. He has experience in the entertainment sector with ABC TV (Walt Disney Company) and PBS. Wilmot has served as business coach with the Shona incubator (Uganda), Nailab (Kenya), Village Capital (Kenya) and Pangea/@iBizAfrica (Strathmore University). He is a Young Global Leader Alumnus at the World Economic Forum and served on a Global Agenda Council.
