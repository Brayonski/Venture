FAQ

1.What is an SME?

While there is no universally accepted definition of a small to medium-sized enterprise (SME) the World Bank Group working classification system includes three sub-categories:

  • Micro-enterprise: up to 10 employees; total assets/total annual sales of up to US$100,000, turnover must be in excess of US$400,000, and tangible assets more than US$200,000;
  • Small enterprise: between 10 and 50 employees, total assets/total annual sales between US$100,000 and US$3 million; and
  • Medium-sized enterprise: between 50 and 300 employees, total assets/total annual sales between US$3 million and US$15 million.

Within each African country, there is an official legal definition of an SME. As these definitions dictate the type of government support, investment, and other resources to which the ’business has access, we recommend reviewing the complete list which available in the IFC's report How Do Economies Define Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs)?

VentureLift Africa works with business above the microfinance level. During the verification process, these SMEs proved to have: a viable product or service; the ability to scale thus creating optimal chances for repayment of investments; and the ability through their growth to create sustainable developmental impact such as new skilled and unskilled jobs in their community or region.

2. What is a Startup?

VentureLift Africa defines a startup as a business less than 3 years old that is still establishing any of the following: solution-problem fit, product-market fit and paying customers.

3. What is Crowdsourcing?

Crowdfunding on VentureLift Africa is a structured approach through which verified businesses may receive financial contributions or services from a group of people ordinarily inaccessible. Through our digital platform, we connect people and institutions with the goal of boosting Africa's SME sector. Across the continent, informal and formalized crowdfunding has a long history be it in the form of collectives, chamas, or other networks and institutions which pool together community capital for its members.

Donation crowdfunding involves giving money to businesses on the VentureLift Africa platform without an equity interest or anticipated payback. Donation crowdfunding on the VLA platform provides:

  • Financial capital which may be matched by a bank or other financial institution;
  • Monetary contributions made to help verified SME owners achieve a vital business or development milestone; or
  • Fiscal donations in return for a (pre-sale) product or new service.

  • In the case of Reward crowdfunding, the company will provide a product or service in return for a crowdsourced financial contribution.
